07B · Approved Strategy
Social Media Strategy
The social media strategy has been approved. This section summarizes the approved framework for reference. Execution details are owned by the eCC social team.

Approved Model: "Doctor-Led Authority Brand"

Staff and dermatologists create the raw content. eCC handles ideas, editing, and posting. Primary platform: Instagram. Goal: make Sapphire recognizable, approachable, and authoritative in the DMV market. This is the higher-growth, more authentic strategy — optimized for long-term brand equity and local dominance.

Strategic Position
PillarDetailWhy It Works
People follow people, not clinics Dr. Kunal Angra and Dr. Divya Angra are the face of the brand. Their credentials, specialties, and personalities are the content anchor. Derm accounts that win on Instagram lean heavily on recognizable doctor personalities and approachable education.
Differentiation assets Cosmetic expertise · Skin of color experience · Medical + cosmetic dual capability · Local DMV roots · Fellowship training These are genuine competitive advantages that competitors cannot replicate through content alone.
Content mix 40% Educational · 30% Before & After · 20% Testimonials · 10% Promotional Weighted toward trust-building over selling — appropriate for a medical authority brand.
Content System
6 Content Buckets
01
Doctor/Staff Personality
Make the physicians the face of the brand. Content types: "Derm Myth or Fact," "What I'd never do to my skin," "3 things causing your acne," POV consult-style videos, reaction videos to skincare trends, "Patient asked me…" series.
Dr-createdReels
02
Daily Micro-Content
One staff member becomes "content coordinator." Capture 5–10 vertical clips/day: injectables prep, laser room setup, provider consults, front desk interactions, product shelves, device close-ups, "Day in the life" clips. Upload to shared folder → agency edits into Reels.
Staff-capturedVolume
03
Patient Question Reels
Every consult becomes content research. Turn real patient questions into educational Reels. Categories: Acne · Melasma · Botox myths · Hair loss · Rosacea · SPF education · Skin of color concerns. Positions Sapphire as trustworthy, educational, premium but approachable.
EducationalSEO value
04
Agency-Produced Educational
High-volume content produced entirely by eCC. Uses: animated skincare explainers, motion graphics, AI-assisted voiceovers, trending-style edits, infographic carousels. Examples: "Botox timeline," "What causes melasma?," "Top anti-aging treatments by age." No MD time required.
Agency-ledNo MD time
05
Before & After Assets
Transformation Reels, swipe carousels, split-screen progress videos, testimonial overlays, treatment journey content. Backbone of conversion content. Requires standardized patient consent process. All historical B&As usable — agency works with any age of content.

Conversion-Focused Content
Strong CTA strategy woven into all content: "Book your consult," "DM us SKIN," "Save this for later," "Send this to a friend." Prioritizes: consultation bookings · treatment awareness · credibility · social proof · in-office promotions · new procedures.

Board-Level Recommendation

Sapphire Dermatology enters this engagement with the most durable competitive asset in the DMV dermatology market: two board-certified physicians with fellowship training, dual-location coverage, and demonstrated skin of color expertise — a combination that no marketing spend can replicate for a competitor starting from zero.

The strategic gap is not clinical — it is commercial. Sapphire's current marketing infrastructure does not match the quality of its clinical offering. The practice is acquiring new patients through largely unmeasured channels, retaining them without a structured program, and leaving cosmetic revenue on the table from an existing medical patient base that has never been formally introduced to the cosmetic service line.

The 90-day plan addresses this gap in the correct sequence: establish measurement, activate acquisition, build retention. None of these three initiatives require significant capital or organizational change. They require execution discipline.

  • 2. 15–20% lift in returning patient visits from email activation by Day 75
